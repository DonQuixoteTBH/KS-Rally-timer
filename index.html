App Test Guide

Rally Timer & Sync: Android User Guide

Welcome to the Rally Timer & Sync application. This guide is designed for Squad Leaders and members to ensure perfect coordination and "Atomic" precision during missions.

1. Initial Setup & Permissions

To function as a persistent tactical tool, the app requires two critical configurations during the first launch:

  • Display Over Other Apps (Overlay): This is the most important permission. It allows the Floating Timer to stay visible on your screen even while you are inside a game or using Discord. Without this, the sync interface will not appear.
  • Battery Optimization: To ensure the background sync service isn't "killed" by Android's power management during long sessions, you should set this app to "Don't Optimize" in your phone's Battery settings. This keeps the timer alive and the connection to your squad active.

2. "Atomic" Time Synchronization

Standard phone clocks can drift by several seconds, which is unacceptable for precise rallies.

  • TrueTime (NTP): The app connects directly to the Atomic Clock standard (via pool.ntp.org).
  • Accuracy: When the app initializes, it calculates the exact offset between your phone and the global UTC time. The clock you see in the overlay is synchronized to this standard, ensuring every squad member sees the exact same second, regardless of their local phone settings.

3. The Main Dashboard (Overlay Menu)

Tap the RALLY button in your overlay to expand the tactical dashboard.

Squad & Leadership

  • Squad Unit: Select your unit (e.g., Solo, T1, or T2).
  • Set As Leader: Only one person should be the Leader. When active, any changes you make to the Target Time or Compass are instantly pushed to all squad members (and web dashboard users).
  • Status Indicators: A Green Dot (LINKED) means you are successfully connected to the Firebase sync server. A Red Dot (OFFLINE) indicates a connection issue.

The Compass Grid

The center of the dashboard features a Compass Selection (North, South, East, West, and Castle).

  • Clicking a direction sets the active target.
  • Each direction has its own saved Travel Time (formatted as mm:ss).

Mission Controls

  • Target UTC: Enter the exact time the hit is supposed to land (e.g., 14:30:00).
  • Prep Minutes: Choose between 1M or 5M. This tells the app how much "lead time" you need before the hit for the beep to trigger.
  • Initiate Sync: Once settings are entered, tap this to start the countdown. The button will turn Pink (ACTIVE).

4. The Floating Overlay

The overlay consists of two main components that can be seen over any app:

  1. Atomic Clock (Top Left): Displays the current synced UTC time. It will glow yellow when a countdown is reaching its final moments.
  2. Control Node (Top Right): A compact button labeled RALLY (or ACTIVE during a countdown).
    • Drag & Drop: You can reposition the overlay anywhere on your screen to avoid blocking game UI.
    • Keyboard Dismissal: Tap anywhere on the dark background of the menu to hide the keyboard once you've entered your data.

5. The Beep & Notification Logic

When the countdown reaches zero, the app triggers a multi-sensory alert:

  • Audio (Beep): A high-pitched, 1-second sine wave designed to cut through background noise.
  • Haptic (Vibration): A 1-second vibration pulse (if enabled).
  • Visual (Flash): The Atomic Clock on your screen will flash yellow and pulse for 3 seconds to provide a visual cue in case audio is muted.

6. Best Practices for Audio & Voice Chat

Best Practices: If you are using Discord or a Bluetooth Headset, follow these tips to ensure you never miss a beep:
  • Volume Channels: The beep is sent through the Alarm/Media Stream. If you are in a Discord call, your phone might prioritize "In-Call" volume. Ensure your Media Volume is turned up high.
  • Background Activity: Use the "Stealth Mode" (Silent Loop) feature on the web dashboard or keep the Android app service active to prevent the audio engine from being suspended by the OS while the screen is off or you are switched to another app.
  • Bluetooth Delay: Standard Bluetooth has a slight "wake-up" delay. Because our app uses a Silent Loop in the background, your headset stays "awake," ensuring the beep is heard instantly without the first half-second being cut off.

Web Test Guide

Rally Web Dashboard: User Guide

The Rally Web Dashboard is a high-precision synchronization tool designed to ensure every unit in the squad triggers their "Beep" at the exact same millisecond. It uses Firebase-assisted Atomic Time Sync to override local device clock errors.

1. Initial Connection (Setup View)

When you first load the page, you will see the Sync Access screen.

  • SQUAD UNIT Input: Enter the designation provided by your Leader (e.g., "T1"). This is case-sensitive and links your dashboard to the Leader’s data stream.
  • LINK UNIT Button: This button performs two critical functions:
    1. It establishes the data connection to Firebase.
    2. It provides the "User Gesture" required by mobile browsers (Safari/Chrome) to allow the app to play sounds later.
Note: Once clicked, the setup screen disappears, and the main dashboard initializes.

2. The Main Dashboard Elements

A. Squad Header & Status

  • SQUAD [UNIT]: Confirms which team you are currently linked to.
  • Current Time (HH:MM:SS): This is a Synchronized UTC Clock. It does not use your phone’s internal time; instead, it calculates an "Atomic Offset" from the server. Even if your phone is 5 seconds slow, this clock will show the correct, synchronized time.
  • Status Text:
    • STANDBY: The Leader has not yet started the mission timer.
    • LINKED: The Leader has pushed target data, and your device is actively calculating the countdown.

B. Compass Selection (Target Index)

The grid represents your intended destination.

  • Function: Tap a direction (North, South, East, West) or the Castle (Center) to select your target.
  • Behavior: Selecting a target updates your specific Travel Time for that location. This allows you to store different travel durations for different mission objectives.

C. Travel Time (HH:MM:SS)

  • Input Field: Enter your estimated travel time to the selected target.
  • Behavior: The dashboard automatically formats your input (e.g., typing "0500" becomes 00:05:00).
  • Auto-Save: These times are saved to your phone's local storage. You do not need to re-enter them if you refresh the page or return later.

3. The Countdown & The Beep

  • Countdown Display: When the Leader starts the timer, a red countdown will appear: BEEP IN: MM:SS.hh.
  • High-Precision Decimals: The last two digits (.hh) represent hundredths of a second. This allows for visual confirmation of the countdown's "Atomic" precision.
  • The Beep: A 1-second Sine-wave tone will play the exact moment the countdown reaches 00:00.00.
Note: The Beep is calculated using the Leader's Target Time, minus your Travel Time, minus a Preparation Lead (set by the Leader).

4. Background Sync (The "Stealth" Button)

This is the most important element for users who switch between apps or use Discord.

  • Button: ENABLE BACKGROUND SYNC (turns green when active).
  • Intended Behavior: Mobile browsers normally "freeze" or "sleep" a web page if you switch to another app (like Discord or a game). Clicking this button starts a Silent Audio Loop.
  • Effect: This "tricks" the iPhone into thinking the browser is a Music Player. This prevents the phone from killing the timer, ensuring the Beep still triggers even if the browser is in the background.

5. Critical Best Practices for iPhone Users

Best Practices: To ensure 100% reliability during a mission, users should follow these steps:
  1. Interact Early: You must tap the screen (Link Unit / Select Target) at least once for audio to work.
  2. Enable Background Sync: Always activate this button if you plan to switch apps.
  3. Discord Settings: If you are in a Voice Chat, go to Discord Settings > Voice > Attenuation and set it to 0%. This prevents Discord from making the "Beep" too quiet to hear.
  4. Check Media Volume: Ensure your "Media" volume is up. On iPhones, the volume buttons sometimes only adjust the "Ringer" or "In-Call" volume; ensure the "Music/Media" slider is high.

APK Install Guide

Rally App: Tester Installation Guide

This guide explains how to manually install the Rally App and configure the necessary permissions for the floating timer and atomic sync to function correctly.

Phase 1: Transfer the APK to your Phone

  1. Download the app-debug.apk file provided by the developer.
  2. Transfer the file to your Android device via Discord, Google Drive, Email, or USB cable.
  3. Locate the file in your phone’s Files or Downloads app.

Phase 2: Authorize "Unknown Apps"

Android prevents the installation of apps from outside the Google Play Store by default. You must authorize your browser or file manager to perform the install.

  1. Tap the app-debug.apk file to open it.
  2. A security prompt will likely appear saying: "For your security, your phone is not allowed to install unknown apps from this source."
  3. Tap Settings.
  4. Toggle the switch for Allow from this source to ON.
  5. Press the Back button. You should now see a prompt asking if you want to install the app.
  6. Tap Install.

Phase 3: Handling "Play Protect" Warnings

Because this is a development build, Google Play Protect may flag the app as "Unrecognized."

  1. If a "Blocked by Play Protect" dialog appears, tap More details (usually a small dropdown arrow).
  2. Tap Install anyway.
Note: This warning appears because the app has not yet been scanned and signed by the Google Play Store.

Phase 4: Critical App Permissions

Once the app is installed and opened, you must enable two specific settings for the Rally features to work:

1. Display Over Other Apps (The Overlay)

The floating timer requires permission to draw on top of your game or map.

  1. Open the Rally App.
  2. The app will prompt you to "Allow Display over other apps."
  3. Find Rally App in the list of applications.
  4. Toggle Allow display over other apps to ON.

2. Disable Battery Optimization

Android’s "Battery Saver" mode will kill the background timer service to save power, which will cause the "Beep" to fail.

  1. Go to your phone’s Settings > Apps > Rally App.
  2. Tap Battery.
  3. Select Unrestricted.
Note: This ensures the Atomic Sync and Beep remain active even if the screen is off or you are switched to another app.
